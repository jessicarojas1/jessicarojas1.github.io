Framework Guide

ML2 — Managed (Core + Development)

At ML2 a project establishes and maintains basic discipline. Processes are planned, monitored, and controlled at the project level. Seven Core PAs apply to any organization; Development adds RDM and SAM.

Core (7): CM, GOV, II, MC, MPM, PLAN, PQA

Development (2): RDM (requirements management), SAM (supplier agreements)

WM: Workforce Management spans ML2 and ML3.

ML3 — Defined (Core + Development)

At ML3 standard processes are established organization-wide. Projects tailor standard processes rather than defining them from scratch. Seven Core PAs and four Development PAs are added.

Core (7): CAR, DAR, EST, OT, PAD, PCM, RSK

Development (4): TS (technical solution), PI (product integration), VV (verification & validation), PR (peer reviews)

Practice Group Structure

Each Practice Area is divided into Practice Groups (PG 1, PG 2, PG 3…) that represent a logical sequence of activities within the PA. Within each group, individual numbered practices specify the work to perform.

Practice numbers use the format PA X.Y — where PA is the abbreviation, X is the Practice Group number, and Y is the practice sequence within that group.

How to Read a Practice

Practice Statement — the imperative that defines exactly what must be done to satisfy the practice.

Elaboration — additional context explaining the intent, rationale, and what makes the practice meaningful in practice.

Compliance Examples — concrete, real-world artifacts and activities that demonstrate the practice is satisfied during a CMMI appraisal.
