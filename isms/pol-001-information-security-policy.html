1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes the overarching management commitment and strategic direction for information security within the organization. It defines the framework within which the Information Security Management System (ISMS) operates and communicates senior management's dedication to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets.

This policy exists to ensure that information security is treated as a business priority, that adequate resources are allocated to protect information assets, and that all personnel understand their responsibilities in maintaining a secure information environment. It serves as the top-level policy document from which all subordinate information security policies, standards, procedures, and guidelines derive their authority.

This policy satisfies the requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 5.1 (Leadership and commitment), Clause 5.2 (Policy), and Clause 6.2 (Information security objectives and planning to achieve them).

2. Scope

This policy applies to all information assets owned, controlled, processed, stored, or transmitted by the organization, regardless of format (electronic, physical, or verbal). It applies to:

  • All employees, including permanent, part-time, and temporary staff
  • Contractors, consultants, and service providers with access to organizational systems or data
  • Third-party vendors, partners, and suppliers who handle organizational information
  • All information systems, networks, infrastructure, and devices used to process organizational information
  • All physical locations where organizational information is processed, stored, or transmitted
  • Cloud services, outsourced services, and managed service providers engaged by the organization

This policy applies at all times, including outside of normal business hours, and extends to remote work environments and personally-owned devices used for organizational purposes.

3. Policy Statement

Senior management is committed to establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an effective Information Security Management System (ISMS) in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022. The organization recognizes that information is a critical asset and that its protection is essential to maintaining stakeholder trust, ensuring business continuity, and meeting legal and regulatory obligations.

The organization commits to the following:

  • Confidentiality: Information will be accessible only to those who are authorized to access it. Unauthorized disclosure of information will be prevented through appropriate technical and organizational controls.
  • Integrity: The accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods will be safeguarded. Information will be protected from unauthorized modification, corruption, or destruction.
  • Availability: Authorized users will have access to information and associated assets when required. Systems and services will be designed and managed to meet defined availability requirements.
  • Legal and Regulatory Compliance: The organization will identify and comply with all applicable legal, statutory, regulatory, and contractual requirements relating to information security, including data protection legislation, industry-specific regulations, and contractual obligations.
  • Risk Management: Information security risks will be systematically identified, assessed, treated, and monitored using a risk-based approach that aligns with the organization's overall risk management framework.
  • Continual Improvement: The effectiveness of the ISMS will be continually evaluated and improved through internal audits, management reviews, corrective actions, and lessons learned from security incidents.
  • Security Objectives: Measurable information security objectives will be established, communicated, monitored, and reviewed in accordance with this policy. Objectives will consider applicable requirements and the results of risk assessment and risk treatment.
  • Resource Allocation: Adequate resources, including personnel, technology, and financial investment, will be allocated to support the ISMS and achieve information security objectives.

4. Information Security Principles

The following principles underpin all information security activities within the organization:

  1. Confidentiality: Information will be classified according to its sensitivity and protected with controls commensurate with that classification. Access to sensitive information will be restricted to authorized individuals on a need-to-know basis. All personnel handling confidential or restricted information are required to maintain its secrecy.
  2. Integrity: Systems and processes will be designed to detect and prevent unauthorized or accidental modification of information. Data validation, change management controls, and audit logging will be employed to maintain the trustworthiness of information throughout its lifecycle.
  3. Availability: Critical information systems and services will be designed with appropriate redundancy, resilience, and recovery capabilities to meet business continuity requirements. Availability requirements will be documented and tested regularly.
  4. Risk-Based Approach: Security controls will be selected and implemented based on a systematic assessment of information security risks. The level of control applied will be proportionate to the assessed risk level, taking into account the likelihood and impact of potential security incidents.
  5. Defense in Depth: Multiple layers of security controls will be applied across people, processes, and technology to ensure that no single point of failure can compromise the security of information. Compensating controls will be implemented where primary controls are insufficient or unavailable.
  6. Least Privilege: Users, systems, and processes will be granted only the minimum level of access rights necessary to perform their authorized functions. Privilege will be reviewed regularly and revoked when no longer required.
  7. Accountability: All access to and actions taken on information systems will be attributable to an identified individual. Audit logs will be maintained and protected to support incident investigation, compliance monitoring, and accountability. Personnel will be held accountable for their information security obligations.
  8. Continual Improvement: The ISMS will not be treated as a static set of controls. The organization will foster a culture of continual improvement, using metrics, audits, incidents, and stakeholder feedback to drive ongoing enhancement of security controls and practices.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

Information security is a shared responsibility. The following roles carry specific obligations:

5.1 Chief Information Security Officer / Information Security Manager

  • Develop, implement, and maintain the ISMS in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022
  • Report on the performance of the ISMS to senior management
  • Coordinate information security risk assessments and risk treatment activities
  • Maintain and update information security policies, standards, and procedures
  • Manage information security incidents and coordinate response activities
  • Ensure information security awareness training is developed and delivered
  • Liaise with regulatory bodies, auditors, and third parties on security matters

5.2 Senior Management

  • Demonstrate visible leadership and commitment to information security
  • Approve and endorse information security policies
  • Ensure information security objectives are established and aligned with organizational strategy
  • Allocate adequate resources for the effective operation of the ISMS
  • Participate in management review of the ISMS at planned intervals
  • Promote a culture of information security across the organization

5.3 System and Asset Owners

  • Maintain responsibility for the security of assigned information systems and assets
  • Classify information assets in accordance with the Data Classification Policy (POL-005)
  • Approve access to systems and data under their ownership
  • Participate in periodic access reviews for their systems
  • Report security vulnerabilities and incidents involving their systems
  • Ensure systems under their ownership are maintained in compliance with applicable security standards

5.4 All Staff (Employees, Contractors, and Third Parties)

  • Read, understand, and comply with this policy and all subordinate information security policies
  • Complete mandatory information security awareness training as required
  • Report suspected security incidents, vulnerabilities, or policy violations promptly
  • Use organizational information systems only for authorized purposes
  • Protect credentials and not share access with unauthorized individuals
  • Handle information according to its classification level

5.5 IT Department

  • Implement and maintain technical security controls in accordance with security policies and standards
  • Manage system patching, vulnerability remediation, and configuration management
  • Administer user account provisioning and deprovisioning in accordance with the Access Control Policy (POL-003)
  • Monitor systems and networks for security events and anomalies
  • Maintain audit logs and ensure their integrity and availability for investigation purposes
  • Support incident response activities and implement technical remediation actions

6. Information Security Objectives

The organization is committed to establishing measurable information security objectives that are consistent with this policy. Information security objectives will:

  • Be consistent with the organization's overall strategic and business objectives
  • Take into account applicable information security requirements and the results of risk assessment and risk treatment
  • Be measurable (where practicable) and monitored at defined intervals
  • Be communicated to relevant personnel
  • Be reviewed and updated as appropriate

Information security objectives will be documented, assigned to responsible owners, and tracked for progress. Formal objectives are maintained in the Information Security Objectives Register (POL-017). Objectives will be reported to senior management as part of the periodic management review process.

Examples of objective categories include, but are not limited to: reduction of security incidents, improvement of vulnerability remediation timelines, completion rates for security awareness training, and system availability targets for critical services.

7. Compliance

Compliance with this policy and all subordinate information security policies is mandatory for all individuals within scope. The organization will monitor compliance through technical controls, audits, management reviews, and reporting mechanisms.

Any individual who violates this policy or any related information security policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract. Violations that constitute criminal activity may be referred to law enforcement authorities. Third parties in violation of their contractual information security obligations may have their access revoked and may be subject to contractual remedies.

Requests for exceptions to this policy must be submitted in writing to the Information Security Manager for review and approval. Exceptions will only be granted where compensating controls are in place and the residual risk is accepted by an appropriate authority. All approved exceptions will be documented and time-limited.

Compliance obligations are further detailed in the Compliance and Legal Requirements Policy (POL-017).

8. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed at least annually, or more frequently in response to significant changes including:

  • Changes in the organization's business objectives, structure, or operations
  • Changes in the threat landscape or information security risk environment
  • Significant security incidents or audit findings
  • Changes in applicable legal, regulatory, or contractual requirements
  • Introduction of new technologies or significant changes to existing systems

Reviews will be conducted by the Information Security Manager with input from relevant stakeholders. Any revisions to this policy must be approved by Senior Management before becoming effective. All personnel will be notified of material changes to this policy.
