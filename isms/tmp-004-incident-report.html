Section 2 — Incident Classification

Select the severity level that best matches the current known impact. Severity may be escalated or de-escalated as the incident progresses. Document any reclassification in the containment action log (Section 6).

2a. Severity Level

Select Level Criteria Response SLA
Active data breach with confirmed exfiltration; ransomware actively spreading across network; complete compromise of critical business system; critical infrastructure down affecting all users; nation-state level attack confirmed. Immediate response. Incident Lead notified within 15 minutes. Senior management and CISO within 30 minutes.
Suspected data breach (unconfirmed exfiltration); significant disruption to one or more critical business systems; targeted attack detected and contained; ransomware on isolated host; insider threat confirmed. Response within 1 hour. ISM notified within 2 hours. Status update every 4 hours.
Contained malware on single endpoint with no lateral movement; policy violation with moderate business impact; phishing attempt with no confirmed credential compromise; unauthorized access attempt blocked by controls. Response within 4 hours during business hours. Status update within 24 hours.
Minor policy violation with no business impact; failed brute-force or scan attempt blocked by perimeter controls; isolated anomaly with benign explanation; lost non-sensitive asset. Response within 2 business days. Logged and monitored for recurrence.

Section 8 — Root Cause Analysis

Complete this section during or immediately after containment. A thorough root cause analysis is essential for preventing recurrence. Use the 5-Whys technique or equivalent. This section feeds directly into the Corrective Action Request (TMP-005).

Section 9 — External Notification Requirements

Review all applicable notification obligations immediately upon classification as a potential data breach. Notification deadlines are typically calculated from the time the organisation became aware of the breach, not from the time of initial occurrence. Seek legal counsel if uncertain about applicability.

GDPR Reminder: Article 33 requires notification to the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach, unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms. If notification cannot be made within 72 hours, it must be accompanied by reasons for the delay.

Section 10 — Recovery Actions

Recovery actions restore affected systems and services to normal, secure operation. Do not restore systems from potentially compromised backups without first verifying backup integrity. All restored systems must undergo verification testing before being returned to production. Maintain enhanced monitoring for at least 30 days post-recovery.

Section 11 — Post-Incident Review

The post-incident review must be completed within 5 business days of incident closure for P1/P2, or 10 business days for P3/P4. The review should be attended by the Incident Lead, ISM, and representatives of affected teams. Focus on systemic improvements, not individual blame.

Section 12 — Sign-offs and Closure

All sign-offs are required before the incident can be formally closed in the incident register. The Incident Lead confirms accuracy of the report; the ISM confirms all required actions have been taken; CISO/Senior Management confirm acceptance of any residual risk and close the incident for management review purposes.
