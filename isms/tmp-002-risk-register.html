1. Instructions for Use

This Risk Register is the central record of all identified information security risks within the ISMS scope. It must be maintained in accordance with ISO 27001:2022 clauses 6.1.2 (information security risk assessment) and 8.2/8.3 (risk assessment and treatment operations). The register must be reviewed at planned intervals — at minimum annually — and following any significant change to the organization's context, operations, or risk environment.

How to Complete This Register
  • Risk ID: Assign a unique identifier using the format RISK-NNN (e.g., RISK-001).
  • Asset: Reference the asset from the Asset Inventory (TMP-003) that the risk relates to.
  • Inherent Risk: Assess likelihood and impact before any controls are applied. Score each on a 1–5 scale. Multiply for the risk score.
  • Treatment Option: Select one of: Avoid (eliminate the activity), Reduce (implement controls), Transfer (insurance/outsource), Accept (formally document acceptance).
  • Residual Risk: Re-score likelihood and impact after treatment controls are applied or planned. This is the risk the organization accepts.
  • Risk Owner: The person accountable for ensuring treatment actions are completed and the risk is managed.
  • All risks with a residual rating of High or Critical must be escalated to senior management and formally accepted in writing.

2. Risk Rating Key

Inherent and residual risk scores are calculated as: Likelihood (1–5) × Impact (1–5) = Score (1–25).

Score RangeRatingColourAction Required
1 – 6LowLowMonitor; review annually
7 – 12MediumMediumTreat; assign owner; quarterly review
13 – 19HighHighTreat urgently; monthly review; escalate
20 – 25CriticalCriticalImmediate action; executive escalation; weekly review

Likelihood Scale: 1 = Rare, 2 = Unlikely, 3 = Possible, 4 = Likely, 5 = Almost Certain
Impact Scale: 1 = Negligible, 2 = Minor, 3 = Moderate, 4 = Major, 5 = Catastrophic
