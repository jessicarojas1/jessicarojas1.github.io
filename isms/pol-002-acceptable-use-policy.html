1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines the rules and expectations governing how authorized users may use organizational information systems, networks, devices, and data. Its purpose is to protect the organization's information assets from misuse, unauthorized access, and threats arising from inappropriate user behavior.

This policy ensures that users understand their rights and responsibilities when using organizational technology resources, and that the organization's information systems are used in a manner consistent with applicable laws, regulations, and business requirements. It supports the organization's commitment to information security as expressed in the Information Security Policy (POL-001).

2. Scope

This policy applies to all users of organizational information systems and resources, including:

  • Employees (full-time, part-time, and casual)
  • Contractors, consultants, and freelancers
  • Temporary and agency staff
  • Vendors, suppliers, and third-party service providers
  • Volunteers, interns, and work experience placements
  • Any other individual granted access to organizational systems or data

This policy covers all information systems, including but not limited to: computers, laptops, mobile devices, servers, network infrastructure, telephony systems, email, cloud services, SaaS applications, and any other technology resource provided or funded by the organization. It applies regardless of whether systems are accessed from organizational premises, a remote location, or via a personally-owned device.

3. Acceptable Use

Organizational information systems are provided primarily for business purposes. The following guidance applies to specific resource types:

3.1 Company Systems and Devices

Organizational computers, laptops, mobile devices, and other equipment must be used primarily for legitimate business activities. Users are expected to maintain the integrity and security of assigned devices, including keeping them physically secure, not leaving them unattended in public spaces, and applying screen locks when not in use. Users must not modify hardware configurations or install operating system components without IT approval.

3.2 Email

Organizational email accounts are provided for business communication. Users may send and receive emails related to their job functions and may use email for limited personal communication in accordance with Section 5. All email usage is subject to monitoring as described in Section 6. Users must not use organizational email to subscribe to personal mailing lists, conduct personal business, or communicate in a manner that could embarrass or harm the organization.

3.3 Internet Access

Internet access is provided to support business activities. Users may access the internet for job-related research, communication with business contacts, and use of approved cloud services. Access to websites unrelated to business purposes should be kept to a minimum and must not interfere with productivity or consume excessive bandwidth. The organization reserves the right to restrict access to categories of websites deemed inappropriate or harmful to security.

3.4 Mobile Devices

Organizational mobile devices (smartphones, tablets) must be protected with a device PIN or biometric lock. Organizational mobile device management (MDM) software must not be removed or disabled. Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT immediately to enable remote wipe capabilities. Where personal mobile devices are used for work purposes, the BYOD requirements in Section 7 apply.

3.5 Social Media

Users must not disclose confidential or internal organizational information on personal or professional social media platforms. Users representing the organization on social media must do so in accordance with any applicable communications or marketing guidelines. Employees must make clear when opinions expressed on social media are personal and not those of the organization. Users must not use social media in ways that could constitute harassment, discrimination, or reputational harm to the organization or its personnel.

4. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on organizational systems and networks. This list is not exhaustive, and the organization reserves the right to take action against any use deemed harmful, illegal, or contrary to organizational values:

  • Unauthorized access: Attempting to access systems, accounts, data, or network resources for which the user has not been explicitly authorized, including attempting to exploit vulnerabilities or bypass security controls.
  • Unauthorized software installation: Installing, downloading, or executing software that has not been approved by the IT department, including freeware, shareware, browser extensions, scripts, or utilities not on the approved software list.
  • Credential sharing: Sharing usernames, passwords, tokens, or any other authentication credentials with any other person, including colleagues, managers, or IT staff (passwords should never be shared; IT staff will never request passwords).
  • Personal gain and commercial activities: Using organizational systems to conduct personal business, run a second job, generate personal income, or engage in any commercial activity unrelated to organizational functions.
  • Piracy and copyright infringement: Downloading, distributing, or using pirated software, unlicensed media, or any content in violation of intellectual property laws.
  • Inappropriate content: Accessing, storing, transmitting, or distributing content that is pornographic, hateful, discriminatory, harassing, violent, or otherwise offensive or illegal.
  • Circumventing security controls: Disabling antivirus software, firewall rules, content filters, audit logging, MDM profiles, or any other security control; using VPNs, anonymization tools, or proxies to bypass organizational monitoring without authorization.
  • Unauthorized data exfiltration: Copying, transferring, or transmitting organizational data to unauthorized external systems, personal cloud storage accounts, or personal email addresses without business justification and authorization.
  • Network scanning and reconnaissance: Conducting port scans, vulnerability assessments, packet captures, or any other network reconnaissance activities on organizational or external networks without written authorization from the Information Security Manager.
  • Cryptocurrency mining: Using organizational computing resources to mine cryptocurrency or perform any other computationally intensive personal activity.
  • Misrepresentation: Impersonating another user, forging email headers, or otherwise misrepresenting identity or authorization when using organizational systems.
  • Introduction of malware: Knowingly or recklessly introducing malicious software, including viruses, ransomware, spyware, adware, or any other malicious code into organizational systems.
  • Physical security violations: Allowing unauthorized individuals to access secure areas or tailgating into restricted locations; leaving sensitive information visible or unattended.

5. Personal Use

The organization recognizes that incidental personal use of organizational systems is a reasonable expectation. Limited personal use of organizational computers, email, and internet access is permitted, subject to the following conditions:

  • Personal use does not interfere with the user's job responsibilities or productivity
  • Personal use does not consume excessive system resources or bandwidth
  • Personal use does not violate any provision of this policy or any other organizational policy
  • Personal use does not create legal liability or reputational risk for the organization
  • Users do not have an expectation of privacy for any personal activity conducted on organizational systems (see Section 6)

Personal use that is extensive, habitual, or that interferes with business operations is not permitted and may be subject to disciplinary action. The organization reserves the right to restrict or eliminate personal use privileges at any time.

6. Monitoring and Privacy

Users of organizational information systems have no expectation of privacy. The organization reserves the right to monitor, log, inspect, and audit all activity on its systems, networks, and devices at any time and without prior notice, to the extent permitted by applicable law. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Internet browsing history and web traffic
  • Email content, attachments, and metadata
  • File access, creation, modification, and deletion
  • Application usage and system activity logs
  • Network connections, including remote access sessions
  • Content stored on organizational devices or cloud services
  • Keystrokes and screen activity on devices where endpoint monitoring tools are deployed

Monitoring is conducted for legitimate business purposes including security incident detection, compliance verification, investigation of suspected policy violations, and performance of IT operations. Access to monitoring data is restricted to authorized personnel with a legitimate need.

Where required by applicable law, users will be informed of monitoring practices through this policy and any employment or contractor agreements. Users consent to this monitoring as a condition of their access to organizational systems.

7. Remote Work and BYOD

Remote work introduces additional risks to organizational information security. The following requirements apply to all remote workers and to individuals using personally-owned devices (Bring Your Own Device — BYOD) for organizational purposes:

7.1 Remote Access Requirements

  • All remote access to organizational systems must be conducted via the organization's approved Virtual Private Network (VPN) solution
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all remote access connections
  • Remote workers must ensure their home networks are secured with WPA2 or WPA3 encryption; public Wi-Fi must not be used without an active VPN connection
  • Remote sessions must be terminated or locked when not in active use
  • Organizational data must not be stored on unsecured personal devices or personal cloud storage services

7.2 BYOD Security Requirements

  • Personal devices used for work must be running a supported operating system with current security patches applied
  • Endpoint protection software (antivirus/anti-malware) must be installed and active on personal devices used for work
  • Device encryption must be enabled on all personal devices used to access, store, or process organizational data
  • Personal devices must be configured with a PIN, password, or biometric lock with a maximum idle timeout of 5 minutes
  • The organization reserves the right to require installation of MDM profiles or security agents on personal devices used for work, and to remotely wipe organizational data from such devices upon termination or in the event of loss or theft
  • Users must report lost or stolen personal devices used for work to IT immediately

8. Consequences of Violations

Violations of this policy are taken seriously and will be investigated. The consequences of a violation will depend on the nature and severity of the breach, the user's history, and the impact on the organization. Potential consequences include:

  • Verbal or written warning
  • Mandatory additional security awareness training
  • Restriction or revocation of system access privileges
  • Suspension with or without pay (for employees)
  • Termination of employment or contract
  • Civil legal action to recover damages
  • Referral to law enforcement where criminal activity is suspected

The organization will follow applicable HR, employment law, and contractual procedures in conducting investigations and applying disciplinary measures. Suspected violations should be reported to the Information Security Manager or via the organization's incident reporting mechanism. Reports made in good faith will be treated confidentially to the extent possible.

9. Acknowledgment

All users within scope of this policy are required to formally acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Acceptable Use Policy. Acknowledgment must be completed:

  • Prior to being granted access to organizational systems for new employees, contractors, and third parties
  • Annually thereafter for all active users
  • Following any material update to this policy

Acknowledgment is recorded using the User Acceptable Use Acknowledgment Form (TMP-010). Records of acknowledgment are maintained by Human Resources and the Information Security Manager for a minimum of 3 years or for the duration of the individual's engagement, whichever is longer.

Failure to complete the annual acknowledgment may result in suspension of system access until acknowledgment is received.
