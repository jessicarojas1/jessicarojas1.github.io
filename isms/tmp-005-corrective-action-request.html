CAR Header

A Corrective Action Request (CAR) is raised whenever a nonconformity is identified within the ISMS. The purpose of the CAR process is to eliminate the root cause of the nonconformity, not merely to address its symptoms. All CARs must be tracked to closure by the ISM. Due dates are determined by priority: Critical — 14 days; High — 30 days; Medium — 60 days; Low — 90 days.

Section 1 — Nonconformity Description

Be precise and factual. The nonconformity description must clearly identify what requirement was not met and what was observed. Avoid vague language such as "insufficient controls" — specify which control, in which system, at which point in time, with what evidence.

Section 2 — Immediate Containment

Immediate containment actions address the symptoms of the nonconformity to limit current and ongoing impact. These are not corrective actions — they do not eliminate the root cause. Containment must be completed as quickly as practicable, and certainly before the root cause analysis begins. Document every action taken, even if it is later reversed.

Section 3 — Root Cause Analysis

Root cause analysis (RCA) identifies the fundamental reason the nonconformity occurred. A corrective action that only addresses symptoms will allow the nonconformity to recur. The RCA must be thorough enough to provide confidence that, if the corrective actions are implemented, the nonconformity will not recur.

5-Whys Worksheet (complete if 5-Whys method is used)

Start with the problem statement (the nonconformity) and ask "Why?" five times, drilling down to the fundamental cause. Each answer becomes the input to the next "Why?". Stop when you reach a cause that is within the organisation's control to fix.

Why 4? Why did [Answer 3] occur? [ANSWER 4 — fourth-level cause]
Why 5? Why did [Answer 4] occur? [ANSWER 5 — root cause]

Section 4 — Corrective Action Plan

Each action must directly address a specific root cause or contributing factor identified in Section 3. Actions that do not link to a root cause are considered compensating controls, not corrective actions. The plan must be approved by the ISM for Critical and High priority CARs before implementation begins. Status options: Open / In Progress / Complete.

Section 5 — Implementation Evidence

Every action listed in Section 4 must have a corresponding evidence entry in this section. Evidence demonstrates that the action was actually implemented, not merely planned. Acceptable evidence includes updated documents, configuration screenshots, training completion records, test results, revised procedures, and meeting minutes. The ISM must review and verify all evidence before the CAR can proceed to effectiveness review.

Section 6 — Effectiveness Review

The effectiveness review is conducted after all corrective actions have been implemented and sufficient time has elapsed to observe whether the root cause has been eliminated. The review date should be set at a point where evidence of effectiveness can reasonably be gathered — typically 30 to 90 days after implementation, depending on the nature of the control. The reviewer should be independent of the corrective action implementation, where practicable.

Section 7 — CAR Closure

A CAR may only be closed when all actions are complete, implementation evidence has been verified, and the effectiveness review confirms the root cause has been eliminated. The ISM is responsible for formally closing the CAR in the CAR register. Closed CARs must be retained as evidence of continual improvement for a minimum of 3 years, or as specified in the document retention policy.
