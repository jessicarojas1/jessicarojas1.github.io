1. Purpose and Objective

This procedure defines the end-to-end process for assessing, onboarding, monitoring, and offboarding suppliers and third-party service providers. It ensures that all third parties with access to organizational information, systems, or facilities meet minimum security requirements and that associated risks are identified, documented, and managed throughout the supplier relationship lifecycle.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to all new and existing suppliers, vendors, contractors, managed service providers, cloud service providers, and any other third party that:

  • Has access to organizational information assets (any classification level)
  • Processes personal data on behalf of the organization
  • Provides services that are critical to business operations
  • Has physical access to organizational facilities
  • Connects to organizational networks or systems

4. Supplier Tier Classification

All suppliers are classified into one of four tiers based on data access, operational dependency, and replaceability. Tier classification drives assessment depth and review frequency.

5. New Supplier Onboarding Assessment

Step 1 — Supplier Request Submission: The Business Sponsor submits a Supplier Request to the ISM including: supplier name and contact details, description of services, data to be accessed (and classification), systems or facilities access required, business justification, and preliminary tier recommendation.

Step 2 — Desktop Due Diligence: ISM conducts initial desktop research within 5 business days: company background and financial indicators, public security incident history (breach databases, news), certifications held (ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, CSA STAR, FedRAMP), any regulatory or legal actions, sanctions screening against applicable lists.

Step 3 — Tier Assignment and Questionnaire: ISM assigns provisional tier based on Step 1 and 2 findings. Sends Security Questionnaire (TMP-008): Tier 1/2 receive full questionnaire; Tier 3 receives abbreviated version; Tier 4 receives registration form only. Supplier must complete within 10 business days.

Step 4 — Questionnaire Review and Scoring: ISM reviews questionnaire responses, scores against minimum security baseline, identifies gaps and missing evidence. Scores below threshold for tier require remediation plan before approval, or escalation to CISO for risk acceptance.

Step 5 — Formal Assessment (Tier 1/2): For Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers, or those with questionnaire gaps: schedule virtual or on-site assessment. Review policies, procedures, technical control evidence (scan reports, pentest summaries, audit reports). Interview security personnel. Document findings in assessment report.

Step 6 — Risk Rating and Approval Decision: ISM documents assessment findings and assigns risk rating: Low / Medium / High / Critical. Approval decision: Approved, Approved with Conditions (remediation plan within 60 days), or Rejected. Approval routing: Tier 1 requires ISM + CISO sign-off; Tier 2 requires ISM sign-off; Tier 3/4 may be delegated to Security Analyst.

Step 7 — Contractual Requirements: Legal/Procurement ensures all contracts include mandatory security clauses: compliance with applicable security standards, incident notification SLA (24hr for Tier 1, 48hr for Tier 2), right to audit, data protection/DPA where personal data is processed, sub-processor requirements and notification obligations, data return or certified destruction on contract termination, security baseline requirements per supplier tier.

Step 8 — Supplier Register Entry: ISM adds supplier to Supplier Register: name, tier, services provided, data classification accessed, primary security contact, certifications, assessment date, risk rating, next review due, contract expiry date, and status (Active).

Immediate review triggers (all tiers): Reported security incident at supplier, major ownership/merger/acquisition change, loss or suspension of security certification, regulatory enforcement action, credible intelligence of supplier compromise, significant change in data access scope, supplier financial distress indicators.

7. Supplier Incident Management

Step 1 — Notification Receipt: Supplier notifies organization per contractual SLA. ISM receives and logs notification. Assigns tracking ID.

Step 2 — Impact Assessment: ISM assesses organizational impact: Was org data potentially accessed or compromised? Which systems or services were affected? What is the estimated duration of exposure? Is personal data involved (triggering breach notification obligations)?

Step 3 — Invoke Incident Response: If organizational data or systems are potentially affected, invoke PRO-007 Incident Response Procedure. Treat as an organizational incident for purposes of containment, investigation, and notification assessment.

Step 4 — Track Supplier Remediation: Require supplier to provide root cause analysis and remediation plan within 5 business days of incident notification. Track progress against plan. Verify remediation effectiveness before resuming normal supplier access levels.

Step 5 — Post-Incident Supplier Review: Following incident, reassess supplier tier and risk rating. Review contractual terms for adequacy. Consider contract modification, enhanced monitoring, or contract termination for severe or repeated incidents. Document review findings and decisions.

8. Supplier Offboarding

Step 1 — Initiate Offboarding: Business Sponsor initiates offboarding process: 60 days before contract end for Tier 1/2; 30 days for Tier 3/4. ISM coordinates offboarding activities.

Step 2 — Data Return or Destruction: Confirm data return or certified destruction schedule per contract terms. Deadline: within 30 days of contract end. For Confidential and Restricted data: Certificate of Data Destruction required from supplier.

Step 3 — Access Revocation: IT revokes all supplier access on the last day of service (or earlier if contract ends abruptly). Verify: VPN access, application accounts, physical access badges, API keys, and shared credentials. Confirmation documented.

Step 4 — Certification and Documentation: Obtain Certificate of Data Destruction for data classified Confidential and above. File all offboarding documentation. Update Supplier Register status to Offboarded with date.

Step 5 — Lessons Learned: For Tier 1/2 suppliers: conduct lessons-learned review. Document service quality, security performance, and any issues for future reference. Input to TPRM program improvements.

10. Supplier Register

The ISM maintains the Supplier Register. It is reviewed monthly and used as input to the annual management review (TMP-007). Fields: Supplier ID, Name, Category, Tier, Primary Service, Data Classification Accessed, Primary Security Contact, Certifications Held, Last Assessment Date, Risk Rating, Next Review Due, Contract Expiry, DPA Signed (Y/N), Status (Active / Conditional / Under Review / Offboarded).
