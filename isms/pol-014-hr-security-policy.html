1. Purpose

The purpose of this Human Resource Security Policy is to ensure that all personnel — employees, contractors, and third parties — understand their information security responsibilities before, during, and after their engagement with the organization, and that they fulfill those responsibilities throughout the duration of their tenure.

Human beings represent both a critical asset and one of the most significant vulnerability vectors in any organization's information security posture. Social engineering, insider threats, accidental data exposure, and negligent behavior by personnel account for a substantial proportion of security incidents. This policy establishes controls to minimize these risks through careful screening, clear contractual obligations, continuous security education, and disciplined offboarding procedures.

This policy satisfies the requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A controls A.6.1 through A.6.8, covering screening, terms and conditions of employment, information security awareness and training, disciplinary process, responsibilities after termination or change, confidentiality agreements, remote working security, and reporting of information security events.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all personnel who access, process, store, or transmit organizational information or who have access to organizational information systems and facilities, including:

  • Permanent employees: Full-time and part-time staff employed directly by the organization
  • Temporary staff: Agency workers, seasonal employees, and staff employed on fixed-term contracts
  • Contractors and consultants: Individuals engaged through third-party contracts or consultancy agreements who work on-site or remotely
  • Interns and work placement students: Individuals undertaking formal work experience programs
  • Third-party service providers: Individuals employed by managed service providers, outsourcers, or other suppliers who have access to organizational systems or data
  • Board members and executives: Including non-executive directors who may have access to sensitive organizational information

Human Resources (HR) is jointly responsible for implementing this policy in coordination with the Information Security Manager. Where provisions of this policy conflict with employment law in a given jurisdiction, the requirements of applicable law shall take precedence, and the Information Security Manager shall be notified so that compensating controls may be assessed.

3. Pre-Employment Security

3.1 Background Screening Requirements

All candidates for employment shall undergo background screening prior to commencement of employment, commensurate with the sensitivity of the role and in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. The standard background screening program shall include, at minimum, the following checks:

  • Criminal record check: Verification of criminal history in all relevant jurisdictions where the candidate has resided or worked in the past seven (7) years. Positions involving access to sensitive data, financial systems, or vulnerable populations shall require an enhanced criminal record disclosure.
  • Employment history verification: Confirmation of employment history for the previous five (5) years, including dates of employment, job titles, and reason for leaving. Gaps in employment history shall be explained and documented.
  • Education and qualification verification: Confirmation of claimed educational qualifications, professional certifications, and licenses that are materially relevant to the role. Professional licenses (e.g., legal, medical, accounting) shall be verified with the relevant licensing body.
  • Reference checks: A minimum of two (2) professional references from previous employers shall be obtained and verified. References shall not be from personal acquaintances unless no professional references are available, in which case the rationale shall be documented.
  • Identity verification: Verification of identity through government-issued photographic identification. For remote hires, identity verification procedures shall be conducted prior to the issuance of credentials and equipment.
  • Right to work: Verification that the candidate has the legal right to work in the relevant jurisdiction.
  • Credit check: Required for roles with access to financial systems, treasury functions, or positions of financial trust.

3.2 Screening Timelines

Background screening shall be initiated upon offer acceptance and shall be completed before the candidate is granted access to any organizational systems, data, or facilities classified as Confidential or above. In exceptional circumstances where business need requires a candidate to commence work before screening is complete, access shall be restricted to non-sensitive systems and supervised, with written approval from the relevant business unit head and the HR Director. Screening must be completed within 30 days of the start date for all such cases.

3.3 Roles Requiring Enhanced Screening

The following categories of roles shall be subject to enhanced background screening, including more extensive criminal history checks, financial checks, and character references:

  • System administrators and privileged access users (IT, security, database administrators)
  • Information security personnel
  • Finance, treasury, and payroll roles
  • Senior management and executive positions
  • Roles with access to Restricted or Secret classified information
  • Data Protection Officer and privacy roles
  • Roles with unescorted physical access to data centers or server rooms

3.4 Screening Frequency for Existing Staff

Periodic re-screening shall be conducted for personnel in high-trust or privileged access roles at intervals not exceeding three (3) years, or upon promotion to a role requiring enhanced screening. Re-screening shall be conducted in accordance with applicable law and with the individual's informed consent. Additionally, if information comes to light suggesting a significant change in an individual's circumstances or trustworthiness, the HR Director and Information Security Manager shall jointly determine whether an out-of-cycle screening is warranted.

4. Terms and Conditions of Employment

4.1 Security Responsibilities in Employment Agreements

All employment contracts, contractor agreements, and service agreements shall include provisions that clearly define the individual's information security responsibilities. As a minimum, employment agreements shall state that:

  • The individual is required to comply with all organizational information security policies, standards, and procedures
  • Organizational information systems and data shall be used only for authorized business purposes
  • Credentials are personal and must not be shared with others
  • Security incidents, weaknesses, and policy violations must be reported promptly
  • Violations of information security policies may result in disciplinary action, including termination
  • Post-employment obligations regarding confidentiality and intellectual property survive termination of the agreement

4.2 Non-Disclosure and Confidentiality Agreements

All personnel shall sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) or confidentiality undertaking prior to being granted access to organizational information. NDAs shall be executed before the commencement of work and shall survive the termination of employment. The scope, duration, and enforcement provisions of NDAs are further addressed in Section 8 of this policy.

4.3 Acceptable Use Acknowledgment

All personnel shall be required to read and acknowledge the Acceptable Use Policy (POL-002) prior to being granted access to organizational systems. Acknowledgment shall be recorded and stored in the individual's personnel record. Re-acknowledgment shall be required annually and whenever the Acceptable Use Policy is materially updated.

4.4 Consequences of Violations

Employment agreements shall explicitly state that violations of information security obligations may result in disciplinary action proportional to the severity of the violation, up to and including immediate termination of employment or contract. Criminal violations may be referred to law enforcement authorities. The organization reserves the right to pursue civil remedies for losses arising from deliberate or grossly negligent violations.

5. Information Security Awareness and Training

5.1 Mandatory Onboarding Security Training

All new personnel shall complete mandatory security awareness training as part of their onboarding program, prior to being granted unsupervised access to organizational systems containing Confidential or higher classified data. Onboarding training shall cover, at minimum:

  • Overview of the ISMS and the organization's information security policies
  • Data classification and handling requirements
  • Password and credential security
  • Phishing and social engineering awareness
  • Acceptable use of organizational systems
  • Incident reporting procedures and responsibilities
  • Physical security and clean desk policy
  • Remote working security practices
  • Privacy and data protection obligations

Completion of onboarding training shall be tracked and recorded. Individuals who have not completed the required training shall not be granted access to sensitive systems.

5.2 Annual Refresher Requirements

All personnel shall complete annual security awareness refresher training. The training program shall be updated annually to reflect changes in the threat landscape, lessons learned from security incidents, and updates to organizational policies. Annual training completion rates shall be tracked and reported to senior management. A completion rate of 100% is targeted; any individual not completing training within the defined timeframe shall be escalated to their manager.

5.3 Role-Based Training

Personnel in roles with elevated information security responsibilities or privileged system access shall complete additional role-specific training appropriate to their functions. Role-based training requirements include:

  • IT and system administrators: Secure system administration, privilege management, patch management, and incident response procedures
  • Information security personnel: Continuing professional development, threat intelligence, security tooling, and compliance frameworks
  • Software developers: Secure Software Development Lifecycle (SSDLC), OWASP Top 10, and code review practices
  • Finance staff: Fraud awareness, business email compromise (BEC) recognition, and wire transfer verification procedures
  • HR staff: Data protection, handling of personnel records, and GDPR obligations
  • Senior management: Cyber governance, risk reporting, and decision-making frameworks for security incidents

5.4 Phishing Simulation Program

The organization shall operate a phishing simulation program to assess the effectiveness of security awareness training and identify personnel who may require additional coaching. Simulated phishing campaigns shall be conducted at least quarterly, with increasing sophistication over time. Results shall be anonymized for aggregate reporting to senior management. Individuals who repeatedly fail phishing simulations shall receive targeted additional training and coaching. The phishing simulation program shall not be used punitively but as a learning tool.

5.5 Training Records

Training completion records shall be maintained for all personnel for the duration of their employment and for a minimum of three (3) years following termination, in accordance with the Records Retention Schedule. Training records shall be available for inspection during audits. Template TMP-010 (Security Awareness Training Record) shall be used to document training completion.

6. Disciplinary Process

6.1 Graduated Discipline

The organization shall apply a proportionate disciplinary process for information security policy violations. Disciplinary measures shall be graduated based on the severity of the violation, the degree of intent (deliberate, negligent, or accidental), the sensitivity of information involved, and the individual's disciplinary history. The graduated discipline framework is as follows:

  • Level 1 — Verbal Warning: Applied for minor, first-time, accidental violations with no material impact (e.g., failure to lock a workstation screen, minor acceptable use policy breach). The individual shall be counseled and required to complete additional training.
  • Level 2 — Formal Written Warning: Applied for repeated minor violations, moderate violations, or negligent behavior that creates measurable risk. A written warning shall be placed on the individual's personnel file with defined expectations for improvement.
  • Level 3 — Final Written Warning / Suspension: Applied for serious violations, including unauthorized access to data, failure to report a known incident, or violations that cause quantifiable harm. Temporary suspension pending investigation may be applied.
  • Level 4 — Termination: Applied for deliberate, malicious, or gross misconduct violations including unauthorized exfiltration of data, sabotage of systems, deliberate bypassing of security controls, or violations constituting criminal conduct.

6.2 Documented Process

All disciplinary proceedings shall be conducted in accordance with the organization's HR Disciplinary Procedure and applicable employment law. The Information Security Manager shall provide technical evidence and expert input to HR in support of disciplinary proceedings involving information security violations. All disciplinary actions shall be documented and retained in the individual's personnel file.

6.3 Escalation Path

Disciplinary cases involving suspected criminal conduct, significant data breach, or material financial loss shall be immediately escalated to senior management and legal counsel. The Information Security Manager shall preserve all relevant evidence in accordance with forensic best practices to support any potential investigation or legal proceedings.

6.4 Termination Trigger Conditions

The following conditions shall be considered grounds for immediate termination without prior warning, subject to the requirements of applicable employment law:

  • Deliberate unauthorized access to, or exfiltration of, organizational data
  • Intentional destruction or alteration of organizational data or systems
  • Sharing credentials or access with unauthorized parties for personal gain or malicious purpose
  • Installation of malicious software or unauthorized surveillance tools
  • Sale, disclosure, or misuse of organizational confidential information
  • Any conduct constituting a criminal offense under applicable law

7. Responsibilities After Termination or Change

7.1 Immediate Access Revocation on Termination

Upon notification of the termination of an employee, contractor, or third-party's engagement — whether voluntary or involuntary — all logical and physical access rights shall be revoked immediately, or at the agreed effective termination date if earlier access revocation is not required. For involuntary terminations, disciplinary dismissals, or situations where there is a risk of malicious insider activity, access shall be revoked at or before the point of notification to the individual. The following access revocation actions shall be completed:

  • Disabling of Active Directory / identity provider accounts
  • Revocation of access to all cloud services and SaaS applications
  • Invalidation of VPN credentials and certificates
  • Revocation of physical access badges and tokens
  • Transfer of email account to read-only or forwarding mode as appropriate
  • Revocation of any API keys, service accounts, or shared credentials held by the individual
  • Return or remote wipe of mobile devices enrolled under MDM

IT shall complete access revocation within four (4) hours of receiving notification from HR for standard departures, and immediately (within 30 minutes) for involuntary or high-risk departures.

7.2 Exit Interview Security Component

HR shall conduct an exit interview for all departing employees that includes a security component covering:

  • Reminder of ongoing confidentiality and NDA obligations
  • Return of all organizational assets and data
  • Confirmation that no organizational data has been copied to personal devices or cloud accounts
  • Notification of any known security vulnerabilities or concerns

The Information Security Manager or a nominated representative may attend exit interviews for personnel in privileged access roles.

7.3 Asset Return Process

All organizational assets, including laptops, mobile devices, access cards, security tokens, documents, and any other organizational property, shall be returned by the departing individual on or before their last working day. IT shall inspect returned devices to verify that organizational data has been appropriately handled and that devices have been wiped or are ready for redeployment. Failure to return organizational assets may result in deductions from final pay where permitted by law, or legal action.

7.4 Post-Employment Obligations

Post-employment obligations, including those relating to confidentiality, non-solicitation of customers and employees, and intellectual property, shall be enforceable beyond the termination date as specified in the employment contract and NDA. Departing employees shall be reminded in writing of these obligations on their last working day.

7.5 Departing Employee Checklist

HR shall maintain and complete a structured Departing Employee Checklist for each termination, capturing confirmation that all required steps (access revocation, asset return, exit interview, NDA reminder) have been completed. The completed checklist shall be retained in the individual's personnel file for a minimum of seven (7) years.

7.6 Change of Role

When an employee changes role or department, access rights shall be reviewed immediately. Access no longer required for the new role shall be revoked within five (5) business days of the change taking effect. New access rights appropriate to the new role shall be provisioned through the standard access request and approval process.

8. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreements

8.1 NDA Requirements

All personnel, contractors, and third parties who are exposed to organizational confidential information shall execute a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) or equivalent confidentiality undertaking before they access any such information. NDAs shall be reviewed and approved by legal counsel and shall be updated when the legal framework or organizational requirements change materially.

8.2 Scope of Confidentiality Obligations

NDAs shall cover all organizational information classified as Internal, Confidential, or Restricted, as defined in the Data Classification Policy (POL-005). The scope shall include, but not be limited to, customer data, financial information, strategic plans, intellectual property, technical designs, source code, security configurations, and personnel information.

8.3 Duration

Confidentiality obligations shall survive the termination of employment or contract for a minimum period of three (3) years for standard confidential information. For trade secrets and proprietary intellectual property, obligations shall continue for as long as the information remains confidential.

8.4 Enforcement

Breach of confidentiality obligations shall be treated as a serious disciplinary matter and may result in termination of employment or contract, civil legal action, and reporting to relevant authorities where criminal conduct is involved. The organization shall maintain the right to seek injunctive relief to prevent ongoing or threatened disclosure of confidential information.

9. Remote Working Security

9.1 Remote Work Risk Assessment

Before authorizing remote working arrangements, the organization shall conduct a risk assessment considering the sensitivity of information to be accessed, the security of the proposed remote working environment, applicable legal requirements regarding cross-border data access, and the ability to implement required security controls. Remote working arrangements shall be documented in a formal remote working agreement.

9.2 Secure Home Network Requirements

Personnel working remotely are responsible for ensuring that their home or remote working environment meets minimum security standards, including:

  • Use of a password-protected, WPA2 or WPA3 encrypted Wi-Fi network
  • Separation of work devices from household or IoT devices where practical, using a dedicated SSID or VLAN
  • Ensuring the home router firmware is kept up to date
  • Using the organization's VPN for all access to internal resources
  • Not using public or shared Wi-Fi for access to organizational systems without VPN protection
  • Maintaining a clear desk policy in the remote working environment to prevent accidental disclosure of confidential information to household members or visitors

9.3 Visitor Restrictions

Remote workers shall take reasonable precautions to prevent household members, visitors, or other unauthorized individuals from viewing or accessing organizational information on screens or documents, or overhearing sensitive business conversations. Organizational information shall not be discussed in public spaces. Video conferencing calls involving Confidential information shall be conducted in a private location.

10. Reporting of Information Security Events

10.1 Obligation to Report

All personnel have an affirmative duty to report actual or suspected information security events, weaknesses, near-misses, and policy violations as quickly as possible. Early reporting enables rapid containment, minimizes impact, and facilitates investigation. Delayed or withheld reporting may itself constitute a disciplinary matter.

Events that must be reported include, but are not limited to:

  • Loss or theft of any device containing organizational data
  • Suspected phishing email received or clicked
  • Suspected malware or unusual system behaviour
  • Accidental disclosure of confidential or personal data to an unauthorized party
  • Unauthorized access to physical areas or systems
  • Suspicious requests for credentials or organizational information
  • Observed policy violations by other personnel
  • Any event that may constitute a data breach under applicable law

10.2 How to Report

Security events shall be reported through the following channels:

  • Primary: Email to the Information Security team at the designated security reporting address
  • Secondary: IT Helpdesk for technical incidents requiring immediate assistance
  • Emergency: Direct telephone contact with the Information Security Manager or on-call security contact for critical incidents outside business hours
  • Anonymous: Via the organization's confidential reporting mechanism (see Section 10.4)

All reported events shall be logged, triaged, and managed in accordance with the Incident Management Policy (POL-010). The reporter shall be acknowledged within four (4) business hours of submission.

10.3 Protection from Retaliation

The organization is committed to protecting individuals who report information security events or suspected violations in good faith. No individual shall face retaliation, penalization, or adverse treatment for reporting a genuine concern about information security. Any individual who believes they have been subjected to retaliation for a security report shall report this to HR immediately.

10.4 Anonymous Reporting

An anonymous reporting mechanism shall be made available to allow personnel to report security concerns without identifying themselves. Anonymous reports shall be treated with the same seriousness as identified reports and investigated appropriately. The anonymization mechanism shall prevent the identification of the reporter.
