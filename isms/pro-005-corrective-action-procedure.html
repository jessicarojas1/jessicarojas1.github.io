1. Purpose and Objective

This procedure defines the process for identifying, documenting, analysing, and resolving nonconformities within the Information Security Management System (ISMS). Its primary objectives are to:

  • Ensure that nonconformities are addressed in a timely and structured manner.
  • Eliminate the root causes of nonconformities to prevent recurrence.
  • Drive continual improvement of the ISMS in alignment with ISO 27001:2022 clause 10.
  • Provide a documented, auditable trail of nonconformity identification, treatment, and closure.

This procedure supports the organisation's commitment to maintaining an effective ISMS that meets both certification requirements and operational security objectives.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to all ISMS nonconformities identified through any source, including but not limited to:

  • Internal ISMS audits.
  • External audits (certification body, customer, regulatory).
  • Management review findings and performance gap analysis.
  • Security incident post-incident reviews.
  • Technical vulnerability assessments and penetration testing.
  • Supplier assessments and third-party audit reports.
  • Continuous monitoring alerts and SLA breach reports.
  • Self-identification by staff, managers, or process owners.

3. Definitions

TermDefinition
Nonconformity (NC) Non-fulfilment of a requirement — either a requirement of ISO 27001:2022, an ISMS policy, procedure, or control.
Major NC The absence of a required control or systematic control failure; a failure that materially undermines the effectiveness of the ISMS; or a repeated minor NC in the same area within 12 months.
Minor NC An isolated, limited-scope failure that does not systematically undermine the ISMS. Deviations from process with no evidence of systemic breakdown.
Observation / OFI An opportunity for improvement — not a nonconformity, but a weakness or inefficiency worthy of attention and action.
Root Cause Analysis (RCA) A structured method used to identify the underlying cause(s) of a nonconformity, rather than treating symptoms.
Corrective Action Action taken to eliminate the root cause of an identified nonconformity to prevent recurrence.
Preventive Action Action taken proactively to eliminate the cause of a potential nonconformity before it occurs.
CAR Corrective Action Request — the formal record documenting a nonconformity and its associated corrective action lifecycle (TMP-005).
CAR ID Unique identifier assigned to each CAR in the format CAR-[YYYY]-[NNN] (e.g., CAR-2025-001).

6. Corrective Action Procedure Steps

Step 1 — Identify and Document
  • The identifier (auditor, staff member, process owner, or ISM) opens a CAR (TMP-005) as soon as a nonconformity is identified.
  • Record a clear, factual description of the nonconformity with objective evidence (screenshots, log extracts, audit findings, policy references).
  • Reference the applicable ISO 27001:2022 clause, Annex A control, or internal policy requirement that has not been met.
  • Assign a unique CAR ID in the format CAR-[YYYY]-[NNN] (e.g., CAR-2025-007).
  • Set the initial priority based on the criteria in Section 5.
  • Notify the ISM within 1 business day of opening the CAR.
Step 2 — Immediate Containment
  • Within the containment timeline (Critical: 24 hrs; High: 48 hrs), implement containment actions to limit the impact of the nonconformity.
  • Containment is not corrective action — it is an immediate, short-term measure to prevent further harm while the root cause is investigated.
  • Document all containment actions, the time they were implemented, and the person responsible.
  • Assess whether the containment is effective and confirm this in writing. If containment is not achieving the intended effect, escalate immediately to the ISM and CISO.
  • Examples of containment: disabling a compromised account, blocking a network path, revoking access, applying an emergency patch, quarantining a system.
Step 3 — Root Cause Analysis

Select the appropriate RCA method based on the complexity and priority of the nonconformity:

  • 5-Whys: Suitable for simple to moderate issues. Ask "why?" at least five times to drill down from symptom to root cause.
  • Fishbone / Ishikawa Diagram: Suitable for complex issues involving multiple contributing factors across people, process, technology, and environment dimensions.
  • Fault Tree Analysis: Suitable for Critical NCs where failure pathways need to be systematically mapped and quantified.

RCA requirements:

  • Document a minimum of 3 levels of "why" even for simple issues — do not stop at the first apparent cause.
  • Clearly distinguish between: the root cause, contributing factors, and symptoms.
  • Determine whether the issue is isolated (limited to one instance or area) or systemic (affecting multiple areas or recurring).
  • Systemic findings require the corrective action scope to be broadened to address all affected areas, not just the instance identified.
Step 4 — Develop Corrective Action Plan
  • For each identified root cause, define a specific corrective action that directly addresses that cause — not the symptom.
  • Actions must be specific, measurable, achievable, and time-bound.
  • Assign a named owner for each action (individual, not a team or role).
  • Set realistic target completion dates consistent with the priority timelines in Section 5.
  • Identify required resources (budget, tools, personnel, training) and any dependencies.
  • Document the complete plan in TMP-005.
  • Obtain ISM written approval for all High and Critical CARs before implementation. Manager approval is sufficient for Medium and Low CARs.
Step 5 — Implement Corrective Actions
  • Execute each action in accordance with the approved plan and target dates.
  • Document evidence of each action upon completion: screenshots, configuration records, test results, updated policy documents, training completion records.
  • Update the CAR status in the CAR Register as each action is completed.
  • Report implementation progress to the ISM:
    • Critical and High CARs: weekly status update.
    • Medium and Low CARs: monthly status update.
  • If an action cannot be completed by the target date, notify the ISM immediately with a revised target date and reason. ISM approval required to extend.
Step 6 — Verify Effectiveness

At the effectiveness review date, the ISM or delegated reviewer assesses whether the corrective action has achieved its intended outcome:

  • The root cause has been eliminated — not merely addressed at symptom level.
  • The nonconformity has not recurred since treatment was implemented.
  • Related areas or processes have been checked for the same or similar weaknesses.
  • Any secondary effects of the corrective action have been evaluated (e.g., new controls not creating new risks).

Document the verification method (re-audit, testing, sampling, observation) and reference evidence. If effectiveness is not confirmed: reopen the CAR, conduct additional or deeper RCA, escalate the priority, and develop a revised corrective action plan. Do not close a CAR that has not been verified as effective.

Step 7 — Close CAR
  • ISM reviews all implementation evidence and the effectiveness confirmation documentation.
  • ISM formally approves closure in the CAR register and in TMP-005.
  • Record the closure date and a reference to the closing evidence package.
  • File the closed CAR (TMP-005 with all supporting evidence) in the document management system or designated secure folder.
  • Retain closed CAR records for a minimum of 3 years for audit and regulatory purposes.

7. Trend Analysis and Preventive Action

The ISM reviews CAR trends on a monthly basis to identify systemic patterns that may indicate broader ISMS weaknesses. Monthly trend analysis covers:

  • NC distribution by source (internal audit, incident, monitoring, etc.).
  • NC distribution by ISO 27001:2022 clause or Annex A control area.
  • Recurrence rate — NCs appearing in the same area in multiple periods.
  • Overdue CARs — actions past target date without approved extension.
  • Average time to close by priority level — trend of improving or deteriorating performance.

Where systemic patterns are identified, the ISM initiates preventive actions targeting the underlying systemic weakness before nonconformities occur. Preventive actions are documented as distinct records in the CAR Register with status tracked to closure.

The monthly trend report summary is presented at the ISMS management review (TMP-007) to support senior management decision-making on ISMS investment and improvement priorities. Leading indicators (e.g., training non-completion rates, patch compliance rates, access review completion rates) are monitored to identify and address emerging risks before they crystallise into nonconformities.

8. Escalation

The following escalation triggers apply:

TriggerEscalation ActionEscalation Target
CAR overdue at implementation deadline (without approved extension) ISM formally escalates with written notice of overdue status and impact assessment. Senior Management
Critical CAR with no confirmed containment after 48 hours ISM escalates immediately with incident status and risk exposure assessment. CISO
Unresolved Major NC from external certification audit approaching re-audit deadline ISM escalates with status report; management briefed on certification risk. CISO and Senior Management; certification body informed if applicable
Effectiveness verification fails on second attempt CAR priority escalated one level; senior management notified; external expert review considered. ISM and Senior Management

9. CAR Register

The CAR Register is maintained by the ISM and serves as the single authoritative record of all corrective action activity. The register must include the following fields for each CAR:

FieldDescription
CAR IDUnique identifier: CAR-[YYYY]-[NNN]
Date OpenedDate the CAR was formally raised
SourceOrigin of the nonconformity (e.g., Internal Audit, Incident, Self-Identification)
PriorityCritical / High / Medium / Low
NC Description SummaryBrief description of the nonconformity and ISO clause or control reference
OwnerNamed individual responsible for driving the CAR to closure
Containment DateDate containment action was confirmed (Critical/High only)
CA Plan DueTarget date for corrective action plan approval
Implementation DueTarget date for all actions to be implemented
Effectiveness Review DueScheduled date for effectiveness verification
StatusOpen / In Progress (Containment) / In Progress (RCA) / In Progress (Implementation) / Pending Effectiveness Review / Closed
Closed DateDate ISM approved closure

A monthly aging report is produced from the CAR Register and presented to management, showing all open CARs by priority, age, and days until target dates.
