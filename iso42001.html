Controls to mitigate identified harms; residual risk acceptance; monitoring and review cadence

ISO 42001 & EU AI Act — How they complement each other
The EU AI Act (in force August 2024) mandates obligations for AI providers and deployers. ISO 42001 provides a management system framework that supports meeting many of those obligations.
Risk Classification
EU AI Act classifies AI systems as Unacceptable, High-Risk, Limited-Risk, or Minimal-Risk. ISO 42001's impact assessment process (A.5.2) supports the risk evaluation required before placing a system on the market.
High-Risk AI Requirements
High-risk AI systems need risk management (ISO 42001 A.5.x), data governance (A.7.x), technical documentation (A.6.x), human oversight (A.9.3), accuracy/robustness/cybersecurity controls (A.6.5, A.9.2).
Transparency Obligations
EU AI Act requires disclosure when interacting with AI (chatbots, deepfakes). ISO 42001 A.8.2, A.8.4, A.8.5 establish external communication, instructions for use, and limitation disclosure controls.
