1. Purpose & Scope

This procedure defines the end-to-end process for evaluating, approving, deploying, and monitoring AI tools. It applies to any AI tool request submitted by organizational personnel and covers all phases from initial intake through decommissioning.

This procedure supports compliance with CMMC 2.0, NIST SP 800-171, DFARS 252.204-7012, and ISO/IEC 27001:2022, ensuring that AI tool adoption decisions are risk-informed, documented, and auditable.

2. Prerequisites & Inputs

  • Requestor has identified a specific business need that an AI tool addresses
  • At least one candidate AI tool/vendor has been identified
  • Program Manager or Business Unit Lead has endorsed the request
  • Relevant documents: POL-AI-001, TMP-AI-001, TMP-AI-002, TMP-AI-003

3. Process Steps

Target timeline: 15–45 business days depending on data classification and complexity.

1

Request Submission 1–2 days

Performed by: Business Unit / Requestor

The requestor submits an AI Tool Evaluation Request containing:

  • Tool name, vendor, and version/subscription tier
  • Business justification and use case description
  • List of data types the tool will process (CUI categories, proprietary data, personnel data)
  • Intended user population and access scope
  • Integration points with existing systems
  • Program/contract(s) the tool will support
  • Estimated start date and expected duration of use
Gate 1 — Completeness Check: ISSO reviews the submission for completeness within 2 business days. Incomplete requests are returned to the requestor. Complete requests proceed to Step 2.
2

Data Classification & Regulatory Screening 2–3 days

Performed by: ISSO + Legal/Export Compliance

The ISSO and Legal team review the data types involved and determine:

  • Highest data classification level the tool will encounter
  • Whether any data constitutes CUI (and CUI category per CUI Registry)
  • Whether any data constitutes CDI under DFARS 252.204-7012
  • Whether any data is subject to ITAR (22 CFR Parts 120–130) or EAR (15 CFR Parts 730–774)
  • Whether the tool constitutes a "covered contractor information system" under DFARS
  • Applicable ISO 27001 Annex A controls and CMMC practice domains
Gate 2 — Classification Decision:
  • Non-CUI / Low risk → Proceed to abbreviated evaluation (Steps 3–5)
  • CUI / CDI → Proceed to full evaluation (all steps), CISO approval required
  • ITAR/EAR-controlled data → Legal hold; Technology Control Plan amendment required before proceeding
3

Vendor Security Assessment 5–10 days

Performed by: ISSO + Procurement/Legal

The ISSO sends TMP-AI-003 (Vendor Questionnaire) to the vendor and reviews responses. Assessment covers:

  • Security Certifications: SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, FedRAMP authorization status
  • Data Handling: Data residency, retention/deletion, training data opt-out capability
  • Access Controls: MFA enforcement, RBAC, API key management
  • Encryption: AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.2+ in transit, key management practices
  • Incident Response: Breach notification timelines, forensic preservation capability
  • AI-Specific: Model training practices, output logging, human oversight options
  • Export Controls: ITAR/EAR awareness, foreign national employee access controls
  • Subprocessors: Complete list with data access scope and geographic location
Gate 3 — Vendor Minimum Thresholds: Vendor must meet minimum security thresholds. Critical failures (e.g., no encryption, no incident reporting, foreign data residency for CUI) result in disqualification.
4

Compliance Checklist Completion 3–5 days

Performed by: ISSO

The ISSO completes the TMP-AI-001 (Evaluation Checklist), evaluating the AI tool against:

FrameworkEvaluation AreasThreshold
CMMC L2 All 14 domains (AC, AT, AU, CA, CM, IA, IR, MA, MP, PS, PE, RA, SC, SI) 100% of applicable practices met or compensating controls documented
NIST 800-171 All 110 security requirements in 14 families Score ≥ 110 in SPRS methodology, or POA&M for gaps
DFARS 7012 CDI safeguarding, cloud requirements, incident reporting All requirements met; no gaps for CDI-processing tools
ISO 27001 Annex A: 5.19–5.23, 8.25, 8.30 (supplier and cloud controls) All applicable controls met or formally excepted
AI-Specific Training data controls, prompt injection, human oversight, output review All critical items addressed; medium items have remediation plan
5

Risk Assessment 3–5 days

Performed by: ISSO

The ISSO completes TMP-AI-002 (Risk Assessment), identifying and scoring all relevant threats and vulnerabilities. Risk treatment decisions are documented:

Risk LevelScoreRequired Action
Critical20–25Tool cannot be approved; must be redesigned or use case must be eliminated
High15–19CISO approval required; mandatory compensating controls; timeline for risk reduction

6

Legal & Contractual Review 3–5 days

Performed by: Legal / Contracts

Legal reviews and executes required agreements with the vendor:

  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) — prior to sharing any organizational data with the vendor
  • Data Processing Agreement (DPA) — specifies data handling obligations, purpose limitation, deletion timelines, and breach notification
  • DFARS Flow-Down Clause — where CDI is involved, vendor must accept DFARS 252.204-7012 obligations
  • AI-Specific Addendum — prohibits use of organizational data for model training; specifies output ownership; liability for AI errors
  • Export Control Clause — confirms vendor's awareness of and compliance with ITAR/EAR restrictions
  • Audit Rights — organization's right to audit vendor's security practices and access logs
7

Approval Decision 2–3 days

Performed by: ISSO (standard) / CISO (CUI/CDI/High-risk)

The approving authority reviews the complete evaluation package and makes one of three decisions:

APPROVED
All requirements met; tool added to authorized software list; deployment can proceed
CONDITIONAL APPROVAL
Approved with specific conditions; remediation items must be resolved within stated timeframe
REJECTED
Critical gaps cannot be remediated; request denied; requestor notified with documented rationale
8

Deployment & Technical Controls 5–10 days

Performed by: IT / System Administrator

Upon approval, IT implements the required technical controls before user access is granted:

  • Configure role-based access control (RBAC) aligned to least privilege
  • Enable MFA for all user accounts
  • Integrate with existing SIEM/logging infrastructure; ensure all AI interactions are logged
  • Configure Data Loss Prevention (DLP) rules to monitor and alert on sensitive data in AI prompts
  • Add AI tool to the Configuration Management Database (CMDB) and establish a baseline configuration
  • Configure network segmentation to restrict AI tool traffic to authorized endpoints
  • Establish API key rotation schedule (minimum 90 days)
  • Test and validate security controls before production access
9

User Training & Awareness 2–3 days

Performed by: Training Coordinator / ISSO

Before users are granted access, mandatory training must be completed and documented:

  • AI Tool Acceptable Use training specific to the deployed tool
  • CUI handling rules when using AI tools (if applicable)
  • How to recognize and report prompt injection attempts or anomalous AI behavior
  • Prohibited data types and use cases per POL-AI-001
  • Incident reporting procedure for AI-related security events

Training records must be retained for a minimum of 3 years (CMMC AT.2.056).

10

11

Decommissioning As needed

Performed by: IT + ISSO + Legal

When an AI tool is retired or authorization is revoked:

  • Revoke all user access and API credentials immediately
  • Request vendor confirmation of data deletion including all prompts, outputs, and any training data
  • Obtain written vendor attestation of data destruction within 30 days
  • Retain audit logs per retention schedule (minimum 3 years)
  • Remove tool from authorized software list and CMDB
  • Document lessons learned and update this procedure as needed

4. Known Limitations of AI Tools in Aerospace Environments

The following limitations must be disclosed to users and documented in the evaluation record:

Hallucination & Accuracy

AI tools can generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect outputs. In aerospace, this risk is critical for engineering calculations, regulatory citations, material properties, and safety analyses. All AI-generated technical content must be independently verified.

Knowledge Cutoff

AI language models have training data cutoffs and cannot reflect the latest standards, regulations (e.g., current CMMC rules, updated ITAR lists), or recent threat intelligence. Always verify against current source documents.

Prompt Injection

Malicious actors can embed instructions in documents processed by AI tools to manipulate outputs or exfiltrate data. AI tools should not be used to process untrusted external documents in CUI environments without appropriate sandboxing.

Training Data Contamination

Vendors offering enterprise plans must contractually prohibit use of organizational data for model training. Absence of this guarantee means sensitive data could influence model outputs accessible to competitors or foreign actors.

Auditability & Explainability

AI decision-making processes are often opaque. For regulatory submissions, airworthiness determinations, or safety-critical decisions, AI tools cannot substitute for documented, traceable human engineering judgment.

Export Control Risk

Cloud-based AI tools may process data on infrastructure located in or accessible by personnel in countries subject to export restrictions. Foreign national access to AI training data containing ITAR-controlled information may constitute an unauthorized export.
