1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements and controls for evaluating, approving, deploying, and monitoring Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools within the organization's information systems and operations. Given the organization's role as an aerospace and defense contractor, AI tools present unique risks to Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), Covered Defense Information (CDI), and export-controlled technical data.

This policy ensures that AI tool adoption does not compromise compliance with the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC), NIST SP 800-171, DFARS clause 252.204-7012, ISO/IEC 27001:2022, International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), and Export Administration Regulations (EAR).

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All employees, contractors, subcontractors, and third parties who use or evaluate AI tools on behalf of the organization
  • All AI tools — including but not limited to generative AI, machine learning platforms, AI-assisted coding tools, AI analytics platforms, AI-powered SaaS applications, chatbots, and automated decision-making systems
  • All organizational systems, networks, and endpoints — including cloud environments, on-premises systems, and remote workstations
  • All programs, projects, and contracts that involve CUI, CDI, or export-controlled technical data
Note: AI tools that will process, store, or transmit CUI or CDI require enhanced evaluation and are subject to additional controls as defined in this policy and associated procedures.

4. Policy Statements

4.1 Authorization Requirement

No AI tool shall be deployed, integrated, or used on organizational systems, networks, or for organizational work without prior written authorization from the CISO or designated ISSO. Unauthorized use of AI tools ("Shadow AI") is prohibited and subject to disciplinary action.

4.2 Data Classification Gate

Before any AI tool evaluation begins, the sponsoring business unit shall classify the types of data the tool will process using the organization's data classification scheme. AI tools shall be restricted as follows:

Data ClassificationAI Tool UseAdditional Requirements
Public / Unclassified (Non-CUI)Permitted with approvalStandard evaluation checklist
Internal / ProprietaryPermitted with approvalEnhanced evaluation + DPA required
CUI / CDIRestricted — enhanced reviewFull CMMC/NIST assessment, FedRAMP cloud requirement, CISO approval
Export-Controlled (ITAR/EAR)Restricted — highest scrutinyLegal review, technology control plan amendment, CISO + Legal approval
Classified (SAP/SCI)ProhibitedAI tools are categorically prohibited from processing classified information

4.3 Prohibited Uses

The following uses of AI tools are expressly prohibited:

  • Inputting classified, ITAR-controlled, or EAR-controlled technical data into any AI tool not specifically authorized for such data
  • Using publicly accessible AI services (e.g., consumer-grade chatbots) to process CUI, CDI, proprietary engineering data, or personnel information
  • Allowing AI tools to autonomously execute changes to production systems, flight-critical software, or safety-critical designs without human review and approval
  • Using AI tools to circumvent access controls, audit logging, or configuration management processes
  • Submitting source code containing export-controlled algorithms or cryptographic implementations to external AI coding assistants
  • Using AI tools to generate, interpret, or process weapons technical data without appropriate licensing and authorization

4.4 CUI Handling Requirements for AI Tools

AI tools authorized to process CUI must satisfy all of the following:

  • Data must remain within a FedRAMP Moderate (or equivalent) authorized environment if cloud-based
  • Data submitted to the AI tool must not be used for model training without explicit contractual prohibition from the vendor
  • All interactions with the AI tool that involve CUI must be logged and retained per audit requirements (minimum 3 years)
  • The AI tool must be deployed within or integrated with the organization's CUI enclave
  • Outputs generated by the AI tool using CUI inputs must be treated as CUI until reviewed and declassified by an authorized individual

4.5 Vendor and Supply Chain Requirements

All AI tool vendors must:

  • Complete the organization's AI Vendor Security Questionnaire (TMP-AI-003) prior to evaluation approval
  • Provide evidence of relevant certifications (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, FedRAMP where applicable)
  • Execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) before receiving any organizational data
  • Identify all subprocessors and data storage locations; US-only data residency is required for CUI/CDI processing
  • Attest to compliance with DFARS 252.204-7012 where CDI is involved
  • Notify the organization within 24 hours of any security incident affecting organizational data

4.6 Human Oversight Requirement

AI tools used in engineering, design, safety analysis, or quality assurance contexts must incorporate mandatory human review gates. AI-generated outputs affecting safety-critical systems, airworthiness determinations, or compliance attestations must be reviewed and approved by a qualified human professional before use or submission.

4.7 Incident Reporting

Any suspected or confirmed security incident involving an AI tool — including unintended data disclosure, prompt injection, model output anomalies, or vendor breach — must be reported immediately to the ISSO and escalated per the organization's Incident Response procedure. Incidents involving CDI must be reported to the DoD via the DIBNet portal within 72 hours per DFARS 252.204-7012.

6. Compliance and Enforcement

Compliance with this policy is mandatory. Violations may result in:

  • Immediate revocation of AI tool access
  • Disciplinary action up to and including termination
  • Contract termination for third-party violations
  • Notification to the DoD Contracting Officer where CUI/CDI is involved
  • Referral to legal authorities where ITAR/EAR violations are suspected

Compliance will be verified through annual internal audits, ISSO reviews, and as part of CMMC assessment activities.

7. Policy Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the CISO and must document:

  • The specific policy statement being excepted
  • Business justification for the exception
  • Risk assessment and compensating controls
  • Duration of the exception (maximum 12 months)
  • Approval signatures from CISO and Program Manager

Exceptions are tracked in the Risk Register (TMP-002) and reviewed quarterly.
